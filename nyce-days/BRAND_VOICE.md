# Nyce Days, Brand Voice

The reference for writing any visitor-facing copy: web, email, SMS, social, captions,
metadata. Read this before writing or editing copy. When the owner corrects a line,
add the lesson here so it sticks.

## What Nyce Days is

A DMV-based (DC / Maryland / Virginia) events, community, and creative-marketing brand.
It started in studios and basements among friends and grew into events and content
nationwide. The product is real connection and good nights, not "experiences" in the
corporate sense.

## Voice in one line

Write like the friend who throws the best party in the DMV: warm, confident, low-key
cool, and never corporate.

## Voice attributes

- **Authentic, not polished.** Plain words, real talk. ("Just hanging out. Making things. Making friends.")
- **Confident and brief.** Short sentences. Fragments are fine. Say it once, don't pad.
- **Insider.** The reader is one of "our people." Things "drop here first."
- **Community-first, anti-hype.** Invite, don't sell. "No algorithm in the way."
- **DMV-rooted.** Local pride is part of the identity.

## Tone by context

- **Headlines:** short, declarative, a little swagger. ("What's next." / "Coming soon.")
- **Body:** conversational, specific, no filler.
- **CTAs and confirmations:** action and payoff, not pleasantries. ("You're on the list." / "You're in. Watch your inbox." — not "Thanks for subscribing! We'll be in touch.")
- **Empty and error states:** human and helpful, never a dead end. ("Nothing here yet. Check another category.")

## Mechanics (house style)

- **Capitalization:** Title Case for buttons and headlines ("Join The Movement", "Send Message"). lowercase for nav items and small eyebrow labels ("shop", "active events"). The brand name is always "Nyce Days". The signature is **"Have a nyce day."**, lowercase "nyce", one canonical form everywhere.
- **Punctuation:** No em dashes, ever. Use a period, a comma, or the mid-dot " · " separator. No emoji. Body sentences take periods; buttons and short headings do not.
- **Length:** default to fewer words. If a clause adds nothing, cut it.
- **Facts:** never invent stats, product types, dates, or place names. (The shop will not sell "plates", that was a one-off cookout. Don't recycle old event specifics into evergreen copy.)

## Words and phrases we use

drops · the Nyce List · hear first · first in line · our people · the DMV · slide through ·
real / genuine connection · community · "Have a nyce day."

## Words and phrases to avoid (corporate filler)

leverage · synergy · elevate · curated experiences · seamless · world-class · best-in-class ·
solutions · "general inquiries" · "we'll be in touch" · "reach out" · "don't hesitate to" ·
exclamation-point hype · emoji · anything that reads like a brand deck.

## Before, after (the voice in action)

| Off-voice | On-voice |
| --- | --- |
| Thanks for subscribing! We'll be in touch. | You're in. Watch your inbox. |
| Get in touch... or general inquiries. | Partnerships, events, content, or anything else. |
| Explore our portfolio of events, content creation, and partnership projects. | Events, content, and partnerships. Projects that moved people. |
| No projects found in this category. | Nothing here yet. Check another category. |
| Browse photos and videos from Nyce Days events. | Photos and videos from Nyce Days events. |

## The test

Read it out loud. If it sounds like a friend texting you about something they're hyped on,
ship it. If it sounds like a marketing email or a pitch deck, rewrite it.
